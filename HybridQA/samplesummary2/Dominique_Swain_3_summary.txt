Jeremy John Irons ( born 19 September 1948 ) is an English actor . After receiving classical training at the Bristol Old Vic Theatre School , Irons began his acting career on stage in 1969 and has since appeared in many West End theatre productions , including The Winter 's Tale , Macbeth , Much Ado About Nothing , The Taming of the Shrew , Godspell , Richard II , and Embers . In 1984 , he made his Broadway debut in Tom Stoppard 's The Real Thing and received a Tony Award for Best Actor . Irons 's first major film role came in the romantic drama The French Lieutenant 's Woman ( 1981 ) , for which he received a BAFTA nomination for Best Actor . After starring in dramas , such as Moonlighting ( 1982 ) , Betrayal ( 1983 ) , and The Mission ( 1986 ) , he was praised for portraying twin gynaecologists in David Cronenberg 's psychological thriller Dead Ringers ( 1988 ) .
